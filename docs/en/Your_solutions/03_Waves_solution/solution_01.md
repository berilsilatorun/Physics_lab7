# 1. Wave Properties

**Problem Setup:**
A sound wave has a constant frequency of $f = 440\text{ Hz}$ (Standard pitch A4).
* Speed of sound in air: $v_{\text{air}} = 343\text{ m/s}$
* Speed of sound in water: $v_{\text{water}} = 1482\text{ m/s}$

**Goal:** Find the wavelength ($\lambda$) in both air and water.

*Key Concept: When a wave passes from one medium to another (e.g., from air to water), its speed and wavelength change, but its **frequency remains constant**.*

---

### Part A: Wavelength in Air
We use the fundamental wave equation relating speed, frequency, and wavelength:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

Rearranging the formula to solve for wavelength ($\lambda$):
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

Substitute the values for air:
$$\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78\text{ m}$$

---

### Part B: Wavelength in Water
Using the exact same formula for water (remembering that the frequency $f$ remains $440\text{ Hz}$):
$$\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f}$$

Substitute the values for water:
$$\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37\text{ m}$$

---

**Conclusion:** The wavelength is significantly longer in water ($3.37\text{ m}$) compared to air ($0.78\text{ m}$) because sound travels much faster in denser mediums, while the source frequency remains unchanged.
