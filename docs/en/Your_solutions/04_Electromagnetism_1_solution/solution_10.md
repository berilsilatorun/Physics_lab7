## 10. Lorentz Force acting on a Wire

**Problem:** A straight wire $2.0\text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10\text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is: a) $90^\circ$, b) $45^\circ$, c) $0^\circ$.

### Physical Concept
The magnetic force acting on a current-carrying wire is directly proportional to the current, the length of the wire, the magnetic field strength, and the sine of the angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field.

The general formula is:
$$F_B = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* $I = 10\text{ A}$ (Current)
* $L = 2.0\text{ m}$ (Length)
* $B = 0.5\text{ T}$ (Magnetic field)
* $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the field.

Notice that the maximum force $F_{max}$ occurs when the wire is perpendicular to the field:
$$F_{max} = (10) \cdot (2.0) \cdot (0.5) = 10\text{ N}$$

### Calculations for Each Case

**a) Angle $\theta = 90^\circ$ (Perpendicular)**
$$F_a = F_{max} \cdot \sin(90^\circ)$$
$$F_a = 10 \cdot 1 = 10\text{ N}$$

**b) Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$**
$$F_b = F_{max} \cdot \sin(45^\circ)$$
$$F_b = 10 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = 5\sqrt{2} \approx 7.07\text{ N}$$

**c) Angle $\theta = 0^\circ$ (Parallel)**
When the wire is parallel to the magnetic field, no magnetic force is exerted on it.
$$F_c = F_{max} \cdot \sin(0^\circ)$$
$$F_c = 10 \cdot 0 = 0\text{ N}$$

**Final Results:**
* a) **$10\text{ N}$**
* b) **$7.07\text{ N}$**
* c) **$0\text{ N}$**
