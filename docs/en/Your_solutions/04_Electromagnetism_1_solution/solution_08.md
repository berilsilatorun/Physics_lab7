## 8. Lorentz Force on a Charged Particle

**Problem:** A charged particle with charge $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ and mass $m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ enters a magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$ at a speed of $v = 10^6\text{ m/s}$ perpendicular to the field. What is the magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle?

### Physical Concept
The magnetic force (Lorentz force) acting on a moving point charge in a uniform magnetic field depends on the magnitude of the charge, its velocity, the strength of the magnetic field, and the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector. The mass of the particle affects its acceleration but is not required to calculate the magnetic force itself.

### Mathematical Derivation
The general formula for the magnitude of the magnetic force is:

$$F_B = |q| \cdot v \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where the given values are:
* Charge: $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
* Velocity: $v = 10^6\text{ m/s}$
* Magnetic field: $B = 0.5\text{ T}$
* Mass: $m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ *(Extra information)*
* $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity and the magnetic field. Since the particle enters perpendicularly, $\theta = 90^\circ$.

### Calculations

**Applying the Perpendicular Condition**
When the particle enters perpendicular to the magnetic field:

$$F_B = |q| \cdot v \cdot B \cdot \sin(90^\circ)$$

Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, the formula simplifies to:

$$F_B = (2 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (10^6) \cdot (0.5) \cdot 1$$

First, multiply the coefficients:

$$2 \cdot 0.5 = 1$$

Next, multiply the powers of 10:

$$10^{-19} \cdot 10^6 = 10^{-13}$$

Combining them gives the final force:

$$F_B = 1 \cdot 10^{-13}\text{ N}$$

**Final Result:**
* **$1 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$**
