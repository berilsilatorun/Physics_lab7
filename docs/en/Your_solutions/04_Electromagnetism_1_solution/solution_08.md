## 8. Lorentz Force

**Problem:** A charged particle with charge $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ and mass $m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ enters a magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$ at a speed of $v = 10^6\text{ m/s}$ perpendicular to the field. What is the magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle?

### Physical Concept
The magnetic force (Lorentz force) exerted on a point charge moving through a magnetic field depends on the charge, its velocity, the strength of the magnetic field, and the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector. 
*(Note: The mass of the particle is given in the problem statement but is not required to find the magnitude of the magnetic force. It would only be necessary if we needed to calculate the particle's acceleration or the radius of its circular path).*

### Mathematical Derivation
The magnitude of the magnetic force is given by the formula:
$$F_B = |q| v B \sin(\theta)$$

Since the particle enters the field **perpendicularly**, the angle $\theta$ between the velocity vector $\vec{v}$ and the magnetic field vector $\vec{B}$ is $90^\circ$.
$$\sin(90^\circ) = 1$$

Therefore, the formula simplifies to:
$$F_B = |q| v B$$

### Calculation
Substituting the given values into the equation:
$$F_B = (2 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (10^6) \cdot (0.5)$$
$$F_B = 1 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$$

**Final Result:** The magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle is **$1 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$**.
