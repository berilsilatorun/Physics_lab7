## 2. Electric Potential - Square Configuration

**Problem:** Point charges of $+1\text{C}, -2\text{C}, +3\text{C}, -4\text{C}$ are placed at the corners of a square ($a = 1.0\text{ m}$). Calculate the potential at the center.

### Mathematical Solution
The electric potential $V$ at the center is the algebraic sum of the potentials created by each charge:
$$V_{total} = k_e \sum_{i=1}^{4} \frac{q_i}{r}$$

1. **Distance to center ($r$):**
$$r = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{1.0 \cdot \sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$$

2. **Sum of charges:**
$$\sum q = (+1) + (-2) + (+3) + (-4) = -2\text{ C}$$

3. **Final Calculation:**
$$V_{total} = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot (-2)}{0.707}$$
$$V_{total} \approx -2.54 \times 10^{10}\text{ V}$$
