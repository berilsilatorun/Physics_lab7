# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Solutions

## 1. Coulomb's Law - Square Configuration

**Problem:** Four point charges of $Q = +1.0\text{ C}$ are placed at the corners of a square with side $a = 1.0\text{ m}$. Calculate the force on a charge $q = -2.0\text{ C}$ at the center.

### a) Geometry and Distance
The distance from each corner to the center ($r$) is half of the diagonal of the square.
The diagonal $d$ is:
$$d = a\sqrt{2} = 1.0 \cdot \sqrt{2}\text{ m}$$

The distance $r$ is:
$$r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{ m}$$

The square of the distance is:
$$r^2 = \left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5\text{ m}^2$$

### b) Forces from Individual Charges
The magnitude of the force $F$ exerted by one corner charge $Q$ on the center charge $q$ is given by Coulomb's Law:
$$F = k_e \frac{|Q \cdot q|}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values ($k_e \approx 8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$):
$$F = 8.99 \times 10^9 \frac{1.0 \cdot 2.0}{0.5}$$
$$F = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot 4 = 35.96 \times 10^9\text{ N}$$

### c) Vector Sum and Final Direction
1. The charges are placed at the corners (top-left, top-right, bottom-left, bottom-right).
2. The center charge is negative, so it is **attracted** to all four corners.
3. The force from the **top-left** corner is equal and opposite to the force from the **bottom-right** corner.
4. The force from the **top-right** corner is equal and opposite to the force from the **bottom-left** corner.



### Conclusion
Since all corner charges are identical and placed symmetrically:
* The horizontal components cancel out.
* The vertical components cancel out.

The net electric force $\vec{F}_{net}$ on the center charge is:
$$\vec{F}_{net} = 0\text{ N}$$

**Direction:** The net force has no direction as its magnitude is zero.
