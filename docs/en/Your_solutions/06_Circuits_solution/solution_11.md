## 11. Power & Energy

**Problem:** What is the power dissipated by a $100\,\Omega$ resistor when a voltage of $50\text{ V}$ is applied across it? How much energy is consumed in $5\text{ minutes}$?

### Given Values
Before starting, let's list the known variables and ensure our units are standard:
* Resistance: $R = 100\,\Omega$
* Voltage: $V = 50\text{ V}$
* Time: $t = 5\text{ minutes} = 5 \cdot 60 = 300\text{ seconds}$ *(Time must be in seconds to calculate energy in Joules)*

### Step 1: Calculate the Power Dissipated
Power ($P$) in an electrical circuit can be calculated using the voltage and resistance. The most direct formula is:
$$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$$

Substitute the given values:
$$P = \frac{(50)^2}{100}$$
$$P = \frac{2500}{100}$$
$$P = 25\text{ W}$$
*(Alternatively, you could find current $I = V/R = 0.5\text{ A}$ first, and then use $P = V \cdot I = 50 \cdot 0.5 = 25\text{ W}$, which yields the exact same result).*

### Step 2: Calculate the Energy Consumed
Energy ($E$) is simply the amount of power consumed over a specific period of time. The formula is:
$$E = P \cdot t$$

Substitute the power we just found and the time in seconds:
$$E = 25 \cdot 300$$
$$E = 7500\text{ J}$$

---

### Final Conclusion
* **Power Dissipated:** The resistor dissipates **$25\text{ W}$** (Watts) of power.
* **Energy Consumed:** Over 5 minutes, it consumes **$7500\text{ J}$** (Joules) or **$7.5\text{ kJ}$** of energy.
