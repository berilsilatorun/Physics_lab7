## 7. Capacitors in Parallel

**Problem:** Two capacitors, $C_1=4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2=6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a $10\text{ V}$ battery. What is the total charge stored on the capacitors? What is the total energy stored?

### Given Values
* Capacitor 1: $C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F} = 4 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$
* Capacitor 2: $C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F} = 6 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$
* Voltage: $V = 10\text{ V}$

### Step 1: Calculate the Equivalent Capacitance
When capacitors are connected in parallel, their equivalent capacitance ($C_{eq}$) is simply the algebraic sum of their individual capacitances:
$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$$
$$C_{eq} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} = 10\,\mu\text{F}$$

Converting to Farads for standard calculations:
$$C_{eq} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}$$

### Step 2: Calculate the Total Charge
The total charge ($Q_{total}$) stored in the equivalent capacitor is given by the formula:
$$Q_{total} = C_{eq} \cdot V$$

Substituting the values:
$$Q_{total} = (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot 10$$
$$Q_{total} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}$$
$$Q_{total} = 100\,\mu\text{C}$$

### Step 3: Calculate the Total Energy Stored
The total potential energy ($U$) stored in a capacitor can be calculated using the formula:
$$U = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2$$

Substituting the values:
$$U = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot (10)^2$$
$$U = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot 100$$
$$U = 500 \times 10^{-6}\text{ J}$$

Writing it in standard scientific notation:
$$U = 5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ J}$$

---

### Final Conclusion
* **Total Equivalent Capacitance:** $10\,\mu\text{F}$
* **Total Stored Charge:** $100\,\mu\text{C}$
* **Total Stored Energy:** $5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ J}$
