## 10. Average Current

**Problem:** A lightning bolt transfers a charge of $30\text{ Coulombs}$ to the ground in a time of $2\text{ milliseconds}$. What is the average current of the lightning bolt?

### Physical Concept
Electric current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charge. When a large amount of charge is transferred over a specific time interval, we calculate the **average current** ($I_{avg}$) by dividing the total charge transferred ($\Delta Q$) by the time interval ($\Delta t$).

### Given Values
Before calculating, we must ensure all units are in standard SI format:
* Total Charge: $\Delta Q = 30\text{ C}$
* Time interval: $\Delta t = 2\text{ ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ s} = 0.002\text{ s}$

### Step 1: State the Formula
The formula for average current is:
$$I_{avg} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}$$

### Step 2: Substitute and Calculate
Substitute the given values into the formula:
$$I_{avg} = \frac{30}{2 \times 10^{-3}}$$

To make the division easier, we can bring the power of 10 to the numerator:
$$I_{avg} = \frac{30}{2} \times 10^3$$
$$I_{avg} = 15 \times 10^3\text{ A}$$
$$I_{avg} = 15000\text{ A}$$

---

### Final Conclusion
The average current of the lightning bolt is **$15000\text{ A}$** (or $15\text{ kA}$). This massive value perfectly illustrates why lightning strikes carry such immense destructive power.
