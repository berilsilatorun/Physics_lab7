# Section 2: Gravity and Pendulums (Yerçekimi ve Sarkaçlar)

## 1. Gravitational Dependence (Yerçekimine Bağlılık)

**Question (Soru):** 1. A simple pendulum has a period of 4 seconds on Earth. What would its period be on the Moon ($g_{moon} \approx \frac{1}{6}g_{earth}$)?
2. What is the required length ($L$) of a simple pendulum to have a period of exactly 1 second on Earth?

---

### ### Solution 1: Moon Period (Ay'daki Periyot)

**Step 1: Understand the Relationship (İlişkiyi Kavrayalım)**
The period $T$ is inversely proportional to the square root of $g$: $T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}$.
(Periyot $T$, yerçekimi ivmesinin ($g$) karekökü ile ters orantılıdır.)

**Step 2: Calculate the Ratio (Oranı Hesaplayalım)**
$$\frac{T_{moon}}{T_{earth}} = \frac{\sqrt{g_{earth}}}{\sqrt{g_{moon}}}$$
Since $g_{moon} = \frac{g_{earth}}{6}$, then:
$$\frac{T_{moon}}{4} = \sqrt{6}$$
$$T_{moon} = 4\sqrt{6} \approx 9.80 \text{ s}$$

**Final Answer (Sonuç):** * **English:** The period on the Moon would be approximately **9.80 seconds**.
* **Türkçe:** Ay'daki periyot yaklaşık **9.80 saniye** olacaktır.

---

### ### Solution 2: Required Length on Earth (Dünya'daki Gereken Uzunluk)

**Step 1: Rearrange for L (L için formülü düzenleyelim)**
From $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$, we get:
$$L = \frac{T^2 \cdot g}{4\pi^2}$$

**Step 2: Substitute values (Değerleri yerine koyalım)**
Take $T = 1 \text{ s}$ and $g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$:
$$L = \frac{1^2 \cdot 9.81}{4\pi^2} \approx \frac{9.81}{39.48} \approx 0.248 \text{ m}$$

**Final Answer (Sonuç):** * **English:** The length should be approximately **24.8 cm**.
* **Türkçe:** Sarkacın uzunluğu yaklaşık **24.8 cm** olmalıdır.
