

## 1. Rotational Velocity

**Problem:** Calculate the linear speed (in km/s) of a point on the Earth's equator due to its rotation. Earth's radius $\approx 6378$ km.

### Step 1: Physical Concept
A point on the equator travels exactly one circumference of the Earth in one full day (24 hours). We calculate linear speed $v$ using the formula for uniform circular motion:
$$v = \frac{2\pi R}{T}$$

### Step 2: Calculation
* Radius $R = 6378$ km
* Period $T = 24\text{ hours} = 24 \times 3600\text{ seconds} = 86400\text{ s}$

$$v = \frac{2\pi (6378)}{86400}$$
$$v \approx \frac{40074}{86400} \approx 0.464\text{ km/s}$$

**Conclusion:** The linear speed of a point on the equator is approximately **$0.464\text{ km/s}$**
