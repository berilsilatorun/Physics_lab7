## 5. Escape Velocity

**Problem:** What is the escape velocity from the surface of the Moon? Express the result in km/s and as a fraction of Earth's escape velocity.

### Given Values
* Mass of the Moon ($M_M$): $7.35 \times 10^{22}\text{ kg}$
* Radius of the Moon ($R_M$): $1737\text{ km} = 1.737 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
* Earth's escape velocity ($v_{esc, Earth}$): $11.2\text{ km/s}$
* Gravitational Constant ($G$): $6.674 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

### Step 1: Calculate Lunar Escape Velocity ($v_{esc}$)
The escape velocity is the minimum speed needed for an object to break free from the gravitational attraction of a massive body. The formula is:
$$v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot G \cdot M}{R}}$$

Substituting the given values for the Moon:
$$v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \cdot (7.35 \times 10^{22})}{1.737 \times 10^6}}$$

First, multiply the terms in the numerator:
$$v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{98.1 \times 10^{11}}{1.737 \times 10^6}}$$

Divide the numerator by the denominator:
$$v_{esc} = \sqrt{56.48 \times 10^5} = \sqrt{5.648 \times 10^6}$$

Taking the square root gives the velocity in meters per second:
$$v_{esc} \approx 2376\text{ m/s}$$

Converting this to kilometers per second:
$$v_{esc} = 2.38\text{ km/s}$$

### Step 2: Compare with Earth's Escape Velocity
To find the fraction, we divide the Moon's escape velocity by Earth's escape velocity:
$$\text{Fraction} = \frac{v_{esc, Moon}}{v_{esc, Earth}}$$
$$\text{Fraction} = \frac{2.38}{11.2} \approx 0.21$$

---

### Final Conclusion
* **Lunar Escape Velocity:** **$2.38\text{ km/s}$**
* **Comparison:** The Moon's escape velocity is about **21%** (or roughly one-fifth) of Earth's escape velocity.
