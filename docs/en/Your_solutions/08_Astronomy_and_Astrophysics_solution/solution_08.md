## 8. Interplanetary Travel

**Problem:** How much time would it take to get from Earth to Mars when Mars is at its closest approach ($d = 55 \times 10^6\text{ km}$)?
**a)** For a message sent at the speed of light?
**b)** By a spacecraft traveling at a constant speed of $40,000\text{ km/h}$?
**c)** By an airplane traveling at a constant speed of $900\text{ km/h}$?

### Physical Concept
The relationship between constant speed ($v$), distance ($d$), and time ($t$) is given by the fundamental kinematic equation:
$$t = \frac{d}{v}$$

**Given Distance:**
$$d = 55,000,000\text{ km} = 55 \times 10^6\text{ km}$$

---

### Part A: At the Speed of Light
The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately $c = 300,000\text{ km/s} = 3 \times 10^5\text{ km/s}$.

$$t_a = \frac{d}{c}$$
$$t_a = \frac{55,000,000}{300,000}$$
$$t_a = 183.33\text{ s}$$

Converting seconds into minutes:
$$t_a = \frac{183.33}{60} \approx 3.06\text{ minutes}$$

---

### Part B: By a Spacecraft
The spacecraft travels at a constant speed of $v = 40,000\text{ km/h}$.

$$t_b = \frac{d}{v}$$
$$t_b = \frac{55,000,000}{40,000}$$
$$t_b = 1375\text{ hours}$$

Converting hours into days (24 hours/day):
$$t_b = \frac{1375}{24} \approx 57.3\text{ days}$$

---

### Part C: By an Airplane
A typical commercial airplane travels at $v = 900\text{ km/h}$.

$$t_c = \frac{d}{v}$$
$$t_c = \frac{55,000,000}{900}$$
$$t_c \approx 61111\text{ hours}$$

Converting hours into days, and then into years (assuming 365.25 days/year):
$$t_c\text{ (days)} = \frac{61111}{24} \approx 2546\text{ days}$$
$$t_c\text{ (years)} = \frac{2546}{365.25} \approx 6.97\text{ years}$$

---

### Final Conclusion
* **Speed of Light:** $\approx 3.06\text{ minutes}$
* **Spacecraft:** $\approx 57.3\text{ days}$
* **Airplane:** $\approx 6.97\text{ years}$
