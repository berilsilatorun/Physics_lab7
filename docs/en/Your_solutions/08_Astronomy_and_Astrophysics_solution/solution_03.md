## 3. Microgravity

**Problem:** What is the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) at the altitude of the ISS ($400$ km)? Why do astronauts experience "weightlessness"?

### Step 1: Calculate Gravity at ISS Altitude
Using Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, we find $g$ at distance $r = R_E + h$:
$$g' = \frac{G M_E}{(R_E + h)^2}$$
$$g' = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{(6.778 \times 10^6)^2}$$
$$g' \approx 8.67\text{ m/s}^2$$

**Conclusion:** The gravity at the ISS altitude is **$8.67\text{ m/s}^2$** (about 88% of surface gravity). Astronauts experience "weightlessness" not because there is no gravity, but because they (and the ISS) are in a continuous state of **free fall** towards Earth. Because everything is falling at the same rate, there is no normal force pushing against their feet.
