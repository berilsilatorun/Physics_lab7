## 2. Orbital Mechanics

**Problem:** Calculate the orbital speed of the ISS ($h = 400$ km). Compare it to Earth's orbital speed around the Sun ($d = 150 \times 10^6$ km, $T = 365.25$ days). Which is faster?

### Step 1: ISS Orbital Speed
* Orbital radius $r = R_E + h = 6378 + 400 = 6778\text{ km} = 6.778 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
* Gravitational Constant $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
* Mass of Earth $M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$

$$v_{ISS} = \sqrt{\frac{G M_E}{r}}$$
$$v_{ISS} = \sqrt{\frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{6.778 \times 10^6}} \approx 7660\text{ m/s} = 7.66\text{ km/s}$$

### Step 2: Earth's Orbital Speed
* Orbital radius $r = 150 \times 10^6\text{ km}$
* Period $T = 365.25 \times 24 \times 3600 \approx 3.156 \times 10^7\text{ s}$

$$v_{Earth} = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \frac{2\pi (150 \times 10^6)}{3.156 \times 10^7} \approx 29.8\text{ km/s}$$

**Conclusion:** The Earth's orbital speed around the Sun (**$29.8\text{ km/s}$**) is significantly faster than the ISS's orbital speed around the Earth (**$7.66\text{ km/s}$**).
