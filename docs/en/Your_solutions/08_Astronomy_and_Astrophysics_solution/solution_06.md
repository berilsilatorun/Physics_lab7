## 6. Solar Gravity

**Problem:** Calculate the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of the Sun. By what factor would your weight increase if you could stand on its surface?

### Given Values
* Mass of the Sun ($M_S$): $2 \times 10^{30}\text{ kg}$
* Radius of the Sun ($R_S$): $6.96 \times 10^8\text{ m}$
* Gravitational Constant ($G$): $6.674 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

### Step 1: Calculate Solar Gravity ($g_{Sun}$)
The acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a massive body is calculated using Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$g = \frac{G \cdot M}{R^2}$$

Substituting the given values for the Sun:
$$g_{Sun} = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \cdot (2 \times 10^{30})}{(6.96 \times 10^8)^2}$$

First, we square the radius in the denominator:
$$g_{Sun} = \frac{13.348 \times 10^{19}}{4.844 \times 10^{17}}$$

Dividing the numbers gives the gravitational acceleration on the Sun's surface:
$$g_{Sun} \approx 275.5\text{ m/s}^2$$

### Step 2: Calculate the Weight Factor
To find out how much heavier an object would be on the Sun compared to Earth, we divide the Sun's gravity by Earth's standard surface gravity ($g_{Earth} = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$):
$$\text{Factor} = \frac{g_{Sun}}{g_{Earth}}$$
$$\text{Factor} = \frac{275.5}{9.81} \approx 28$$

---

### Final Conclusion
* **Solar Gravity:** $275.5\text{ m/s}^2$
* **Weight Increase:** If you could stand on the Sun, your weight would increase by a factor of **28**.
